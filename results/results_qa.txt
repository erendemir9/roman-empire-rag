Question: What happened at the Battle of the Teutoburg Forest?
Answer: At the Battle of the Teutoburg Forest in AD 9, three Roman legions led by Publius Quinctilius Varus were ambushed and completely destroyed by a coalition of Germanic tribes. This battle was very shocking to Augustus, who was so upset that he never used those legion numbers again. It made Rome decide not to try and take control of areas east of the Rhine River.

Question: What caused the fall of the Western Roman Empire?
Answer: The fall of the Western Roman Empire was due to a combination of factors including political instability, civil wars, economic difficulties like currency debasement and heavy taxation, sustained military pressure from Germanic peoples along an extremely long frontier that was difficult to defend, and the sheer administrative and logistical challenge of governing such a vast territory with premodern communication and transportation technology.
